Analysis

Google released Gemma 4 today. For freelancers and small AI teams, the economics are worth understanding.

The benchmark context: the 31B model ranks 3rd on the Arena AI text leaderboard; the 26B ranks 6th. Both outperform models 20x their parameter count.

The commercial implication: Apache 2.0 license. Full commercial use permitted. No royalties, no restrictions on client deliverables.

For anyone billing AI-assisted work to clients:

Running Gemma 4 locally via Ollama means the per-token cost is zero. No usage caps, no API rate limits, no vendor dependency in your production stack. For high-volume client work, that's a real line item reduction.

The practical specs: • 256K context window — sufficient for complex multi-step deliverables • Native vision and audio — covers content, creative, and analysis workflows • MoE architecture — only 2–4B parameters active during inference, preserves RAM • Edge variants (E2B/E4B) designed for laptop-class hardware • Available on Ollama, Hugging Face, Kaggle, AI Studio

The unglamorous truth: most freelancers won't replace every API call. But having a local, commercial-use, frontier-tier model as a fallback — or a primary for the right use cases — changes the cost structure of your practice.
