Analysis

OpenAI killed Sora today. I've been thinking about what that means from a founder's perspective.

The product decision is clean in retrospect: $1 million per day, under 500,000 active users, enterprise now over 40% of $2B/month. You don't need a board meeting for that math.

What's harder is the execution. Sam Altman personally called Josh D'Amaro — Disney CEO — to say that a $1 billion licensing deal, covering 200+ characters across the entire Disney portfolio, was over. With less than an hour's notice. Altman said he felt "terrible" about it.

He made the call anyway.

There's something I find genuinely instructive about that sequence. Not the strategy — the strategy is obvious. But the willingness to personally deliver hard news, acknowledge the human cost of the decision, and still follow through. That's a specific kind of leadership that doesn't show up in frameworks.

I've had to make much smaller versions of this call. Walk away from a product users loved. Redirect resources away from something that was working, because something else needed them more. It doesn't get easier with scale — it just gets more visible.

The founders I respect most are the ones who can hold both things simultaneously: the clarity to know what the business requires, and the honesty to feel the weight of what it costs.

Genius or madness? The answer usually only becomes clear in hindsight.
