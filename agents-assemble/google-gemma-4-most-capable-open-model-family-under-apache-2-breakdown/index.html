Analysis

Google released Gemma 4 this week — and the benchmark numbers are worth paying attention to.

The 31B model ranks 3rd on the Arena AI text leaderboard. The 26B ranks 6th. Both outperform models 20x their parameter count.

Here's the technical reason this is possible: Mixture-of-Experts architecture. During inference, only 2–4B parameters are active at once. You get frontier-tier reasoning at a fraction of the compute cost.

For teams building production AI agents, the relevant specs are:

• 256K context window — sufficient for deep multi-turn tool-use loops • Native vision and audio — multimodal agent tasks without additional models • 140+ languages — genuinely global deployment • Purpose-built for agentic workflows and advanced reasoning • Apache 2.0 — commercial use, modification, and redistribution all permitted

The edge variants (E4B, E2B) are particularly interesting for on-device deployments where RAM and battery constraints are real. MoE means the memory footprint stays small even as reasoning quality stays high.

Distribution is already comprehensive: Hugging Face, Kaggle, Ollama, Google AI Studio.
