Analysis

DeepMind published a cognitive framework for measuring AGI milestones this week.

For anyone building AI agents, here's the practical read:

The framework maps AGI progress across 5 independent cognitive domains — perception, reasoning, planning, learning, and social interaction. Independence is the key word. A system (or an agent) can be strong in one domain and weak in another.

For builders, this translates to a gap analysis: - Where does your agent perform reliably? - Where does it degrade under novel conditions? - Which cognitive domain is the actual bottleneck in your use case?

The Kaggle hackathon running alongside the framework release is also notable — DeepMind is explicitly crowdsourcing what AGI benchmarks should measure. The community gets to participate in defining the milestones.

And the AlphaGo retrospective is worth more than nostalgia. The Go → protein folding → drug discovery arc is a case study in domain transfer: the same fundamental approach, applied progressively. If you're iterating on agents, understanding that transfer pattern matters.

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite also ships this week for speed-at-scale production pipelines — the research and the infrastructure are moving in parallel.
