Analysis

OpenAI raised $122B at an $852B valuation. The largest private funding round in history.

I want to talk about the $35B contingency, because I think it's the most interesting part of this deal and nobody's writing about it seriously.

Amazon committed $50B. $35B of that is conditional on either an IPO or an AGI milestone.

Think about what that means structurally. Amazon isn't saying "we believe in OpenAI." Amazon is saying "we want financial exposure to a scenario where OpenAI is successful enough to IPO, OR the scenario where AGI is real." Both of those trigger the $35B. Neither of those requires OpenAI to simply grow well — they require transformative inflection points.

That's not a growth investment. That's a scenario hedge on the two most consequential possible futures for AI.

I find this more interesting than the headline number. The smart money isn't betting on steady compounding. It's betting on discontinuity.

The rest of the numbers are real: — $2B/month revenue — 900M+ weekly users — Enterprise >40% of total — $100M ARR ads pilot in under 6 weeks

The business works. But the investment structure tells you what the sophisticated investors actually think the path looks like.

IPO late 2026. Top 10 US IPO if it lists near $852B.
