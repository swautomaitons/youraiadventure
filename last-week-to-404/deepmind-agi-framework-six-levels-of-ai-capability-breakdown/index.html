Analysis

DeepMind published a cognitive framework for measuring AGI milestones this week.

The framework is genuinely interesting: five cognitive domains (perception, reasoning, planning, learning, social interaction), with the core argument that current benchmarks test specific capabilities rather than generalization across all five.

The simultaneous Kaggle hackathon for community-built benchmarks is the more interesting signal. It suggests that even the lab publishing the framework doesn't think internal benchmark definition is sufficient — external validation is part of the legitimacy of the measurement itself.

Also this week: AlphaGo's 10-year retrospective. The Go → protein folding → drug discovery arc is worth revisiting for anyone thinking about AI capability trajectories. The initial panic about a board game turns out to have been poorly aimed. The downstream impacts were significantly more consequential.

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite also ships for production-scale speed deployments.

Big week for a field that still hasn't settled on a definition of what it's measuring.
