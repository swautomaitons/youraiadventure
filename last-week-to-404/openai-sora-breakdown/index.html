Analysis

OpenAI cancelled a $1 billion Disney deal — and the CEO got less than an hour's warning.

I cover AI news for a living and this one still landed differently.

Josh D'Amaro had 200+ licensed characters on the table (Disney, Marvel, Pixar, Star Wars). The whole portfolio. Less than one hour later, he got the call that it was over.

The reason is entirely rational from a business standpoint: Sora was costing $1 million per day to operate with under 500,000 active users. Enterprise revenue now represents over 40% of OpenAI's $2 billion monthly run rate. GPT-5.5 ("Spud") just finished pretraining.

The math said kill it. So they killed it.

What's interesting here from a strategic lens: this is the most transparent signal yet that OpenAI has moved firmly into enterprise-first mode. Consumer tools exist to generate attention and usage data, not as primary revenue drivers. When the attention-to-cost ratio breaks, the tool goes.

For anyone building on or around AI consumer platforms: the lesson is that compute cost discipline is going to drive product decisions faster than user love ever will.

And for the AI video space specifically: Runway, Kling, and Pika all just got a more interesting competitive landscape overnight.

What's your read — does this signal a broader pullback from consumer AI at the foundation model level?
