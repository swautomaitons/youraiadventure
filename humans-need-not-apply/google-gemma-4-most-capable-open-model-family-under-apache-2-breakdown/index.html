Analysis

Google released Gemma 4 today. The industry implications are worth examining carefully.

The data first:

The 31B model ranks 3rd on the Arena AI text leaderboard. The 26B ranks 6th. Both outperform models 20x their parameter count. Apache 2.0 license. Full commercial use permitted.

The architecture explanation: Mixture-of-Experts with only 2–4B parameters active during inference. Frontier-class reasoning at a fraction of the compute cost. Available locally via Ollama.

Here's the problem this creates:

Enterprise procurement teams now have a credible alternative to closed-model API subscriptions. A model ranked #3 globally, running on-premise, with no per-token costs and no vendor lock-in — that changes every renewal conversation for OpenAI, Anthropic, and Mistral's enterprise sales teams.

The Q2 questions to track:

1. Do closed-model labs reprice proactively, or wait for churn data? 2. Which enterprise verticals move to local LLM deployment first (legal, healthcare, finance)? 3. Does the E2B/E4B edge variant accelerate on-device AI in consumer hardware? 4. How does the Apache 2.0 commercial use clause affect fine-tuning and redistribution plays?

The underlying pattern here is one we've seen before: compute commoditizes, the frontier drops in price, and the businesses built on frontier access need new differentiation.
