Analysis

$122B. The largest private funding round in recorded history.

Three realities nobody wants to admit:

1. AMAZON, NVIDIA, AND SOFTBANK AREN'T BELIEVERS — THEY'RE HEDGERS At this scale, strategic investment isn't about upside. It's about insurance. Each of these companies has existential exposure to AI infrastructure they don't own. This round isn't a vote of confidence in OpenAI's mission — it's a hedge against a future where not having access is catastrophic.

2. THE $35B ASTERISK CHANGES THE HEADLINE $35B of Amazon's $50B commitment is contingent on an IPO or an AGI milestone. The "largest raise ever" story is technically accurate and contextually misleading at the same time.

3. THE BUSINESS IS REAL — AND IT CHANGES THE RISK PROFILE $2B/month in revenue. 900M+ weekly users. Enterprise >40% of total revenue. An ads pilot at $100M ARR in under 6 weeks. This isn't speculative anymore. Which means the valuation debate has shifted: the question is no longer "can they generate revenue?" — it's "does $852B reflect the ceiling or the floor?"

The IPO is expected late 2026. If it lists near this valuation, it enters the top 10 US IPOs of all time.

The companies that will be most affected aren't just the ones investing. They're the ones that aren't.
