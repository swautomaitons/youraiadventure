Gemma 4 delivers a 13x improvement in agentic task performance over Gemma 3, driven by Google's decision to train tool calling as a first-class objective rather than fine-tuning it as an afterthought. Its 89.2% score on AIME 2025 (math reasoning) and 86.4% on agentic benchmarks represent category dominance, not just leadership.

The multimodal dimension is where Gemma 4 stands alone. It is currently the only open-source model at this performance tier with native vision capability. For the growing class of applications that require vision plus reasoning plus tool calling — document understanding, visual QA, multimodal agents — Gemma 4 has no open-source competitor.

The tradeoff is speed. At 25 tokens/second, Gemma 4 trails Qwen 3.5's 35 tokens/second by 40%. For latency-sensitive applications, this gap translates directly into user experience and infrastructure costs.

Qwen 3.5 delivers 40% more throughput than Gemma 4 at 35 tokens/second versus 25 tokens/second. For latency-sensitive applications — chatbots, real-time processing, interactive agents — this gap translates directly into user experience and infrastructure costs. It's not a rounding error.

On reasoning benchmarks, Qwen 3.5 edges Gemma 4 on GPQA (85.5% vs 84.3%) and LiveCodeBench (83.6% vs 80.0%). These aren't decisive margins, but they're consistent. For general-purpose reasoning and code generation, Qwen currently holds a slight advantage.

The critical gap: no multimodal capability. Qwen 3.5, despite strong text benchmarks, doesn't offer equivalent vision performance. For applications requiring visual reasoning, it's simply not an option.

700M Cap

Llama 4 has the largest ecosystem of any open model — more integrations, more tooling, more community support. For developers building on established infrastructure, this matters. But the licensing constraint is a structural ceiling that most comparisons ignore.

The 700M monthly active user cap creates a legal threshold that individual developers won't hit, but that companies, platforms, and SaaS builders must plan around. The cost of renegotiating a license mid-scale is non-trivial, and it's a variable that benchmark comparisons systematically underweight.

On benchmarks, Llama 4 trails both Gemma 4 and Qwen 3.5 across most categories. It's competitive, but not leading. The ecosystem advantage is real, but the licensing disadvantage compounds over time.

Open-source AI is fragmenting, not converging. Organizations that treat model selection as a strategic decision based on workload requirements will have a structural advantage. Licensing is the most underpriced variable.
Key Takeaways
  1. Qwen 3.5 is faster and edges general benchmarks. 35 tokens/second vs Gemma 4's 25 tokens/second. GPQA: 85.5% vs 84.3%. LiveCodeBench: 83.6% vs 80.0%. For speed-critical and code-heavy workloads, Qwen currently leads. These aren't marginal differences on speed — 40% faster throughput changes production economics.
  2. Gemma 4 dominates math and agentic tasks. 89.2% on AIME 2025 (math) and 86.4% on agentic benchmarks — a 13x improvement over Gemma 3. More importantly, Gemma 4 is the only multimodal open model at this performance tier. If your workload requires vision + reasoning + tool calling, there's currently no open-source alternative.
  3. The licensing gap is the most underpriced variable in model selection. Apache 2.0 (Gemma 4 and Qwen 3.5) means zero commercial restrictions. Llama 4's 700M monthly active user cap creates a legal ceiling that compounds as you scale. Most benchmark comparisons ignore licensing entirely. That's a mistake.
  4. Open-source AI is fragmenting, not converging. There is no "best open-source model" in 2026. There are models that lead specific categories. The era of a single dominant open model is over, and strategic model selection based on workload requirements is now mandatory.
  5. Multimodal remains Gemma 4's strongest differentiator. No other open model at this performance tier offers native vision capability. For organizations building multimodal pipelines — document understanding, image analysis, visual reasoning — Gemma 4 is currently the only viable open-source option.
